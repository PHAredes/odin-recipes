Farofa de Banana

Description

Farofa de Banana is a delightful Brazilian side dish that combines the savory taste of toasted manioc flour with the sweet and caramelized flavor of ripe plantain/banana. This unique combination creates a delicious accompaniment with a perfect balance of textures and tastes.

Although it is named after a banana in this recipe, 'Banana da Terra,' a hybrid between dessert banana and cooking plantain, is typically used. If those aren't available, you can substitute with cooking plantain instead

Ingredients

  • 2 cups manioc flour
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 3 ripe plantains, sliced
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the manioc flour to the skillet and stir constantly until it becomes golden brown and has a nutty aroma.
  3. Push the toasted flour to one side of the skillet and add the sliced plantains to the other side.
  4. Sprinkle sugar over the plantains and cook until they caramelize, turning them gently to coat evenly.
  5. Combine the toasted manioc flour with the caramelized plantains in the skillet.
  6. Add raisins, chopped walnuts, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt. Stir well to combine all ingredients.
  7. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  8. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before serving.
  9. Farofa de Banana is a delightful side dish, perfect to accompany a variety of Brazilian meals. Serve and enjoy!
