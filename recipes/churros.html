Churros

Description

Churros are a Spanish treat, renowned for their crispy exterior and tender, doughy interior. These deep-fried delights are often coated in cinnamon sugar, providing a perfect balance of sweetness and spice.

Enjoyed on their own or dipped in chocolate sauce, churros are a beloved indulgence that brings a touch of warmth and nostalgia with every bite.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for frying
  • 1/2 cup white sugar, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Steps

  1. In a small saucepan, combine water, sugar, salt, and 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Remove the saucepan from heat and add the flour, stirring vigorously until the mixture forms a ball.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil for frying in a deep fryer or large pot to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  4. Transfer the dough to a pastry bag fitted with a large star tip. Pipe strips of dough directly into the hot oil, cutting with scissors at the desired length.
  5. Fry the churros until golden brown, turning them with tongs to ensure even cooking. Once done, place them on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  6. In a separate bowl, combine 1/2 cup sugar and cinnamon. Roll the warm churros in the mixture until evenly coated.
  7. Serve the churros on their own or with a side of chocolate sauce for dipping. Enjoy the irresistible combination of crispy, sugary goodness with each delicious bite.
