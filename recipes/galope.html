Galopé

Description

Galopé is a traditional Brazilian dish, known for its rich and hearty flavors. This savory delicacy is a fusion of rooster (galo) and pig's trotters (pé de porco), creating a dish that is both comforting and robust.

The slow-cooked amalgamation of meats, seasoned with a blend of spices, results in a dish that pays homage to the culinary heritage of Northeast region and Minas Gerais, offering a taste of Brazil's diverse and delicious cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole free-range rooster, cut into pieces
  • 2 pig's trotters, cleaned and cut into sections
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped half cilantro
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup water

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until they become fragrant and translucent.
  2. Add the rooster pieces and pig's trotters to the pot, browning them on all sides.
  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes, bell pepper, green onions, and cilantro, allowing the vegetables to soften and release their flavors.
  4. Season the mixture with salt, black pepper, and add the bay leaves for additional depth of flavor.
  5. Pour in the water, ensuring that the meats are partially submerged. Bring the pot to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer, covered, for about 1.5 to 2 hours or until the meats are tender and the flavors meld together.
  6. Adjust the seasoning to taste and remove the bay leaves before serving.
  7. Galopé is traditionally served with rice, beans, and a side of couve (collard greens) and farofa (toasted manioc flour).
